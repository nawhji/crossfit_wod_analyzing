Champions Club Scaling Notes
RANT
"Oh you went platinum, yeah, that's nice / now let me see you do the same thing twice" - Nas
Big fan of a two-rep max in general, and any max involving more than one rep. Coming from a sports background, I think a 2-rep max translates to sports more than one because plays in basketball and football and volleyball don't seem to be single-effort things; linemen got to block the same dude more than once within 7 seconds or so, basketball defender needs two fight over two screens (stop going under or switching screens you pansies!), a block at the volleyball net might lead to another one in a few seconds. Maintaining peak(ish) strength over extended durations is not too far off the definition of fitness.
MAIN DECISION
What constitutes "unbroken"? I've been thinking about this critically in my coaching recently, and I'm starting to believe that doing 1 hang clean, then taking a few breaths with the bar in the hang position before doing the next one is not really unbroken. Might as well set it down - and there's nothing wrong with that.

Spend 20 minutes finding a max height box jump (spotters are nice here; if you don't have one, go highest you can without risk of missing a rep). Practice hang cleans in the warmup
I WANT TO EMPHASIZE THE SQUAT BUT DON'T HAVE THE TECHNIQUE TO DO IT WITH SPEED SCALE
Max effort front squats instead of cleans.
