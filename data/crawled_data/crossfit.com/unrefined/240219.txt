Chris Sinagoga
February 19th, 2024 at 10:28 am
Commented on: 240219
Champions Club Scaling Notes
RANT
Man it is crazy how much CrossFit has evolved in terms of fitness standards. Just take a minute to appreciate what is being asked of today's workout: handstand walks in an 8 minute workout! Do you have any idea how well-rounded and fit you have to be for Diane to be a rough conditioning dose? And now (in kind of a version of Diane if you think about it) upping the skill of handstands? I think it can be easy to get caught in the CrossFit bubble and take our fitness for granted. Most of us can't do what professional athletes do, but I'd bet most of them can't do handstand walks and kettlebell swings at peak intensity either.
SCALING QUESTIONS
Am I dope enough at CrossFit for this thing to be like a 7 out of 10 on intensity?
Do I want to scale for intensity (sub handstand walks for something you can do intense) or scale for skill (keep handstand walks and extend time)?
Am I good enough at handstands to have a set distance or do I need to give al allotted time to practice?
Do I preserve the upside-downness of handstand walks when subbing (hs hold/hspu) or just keep with the shoulder stimulus (push press, strict press, overhead walk)?
NEW TO CROSSFIT SCALE
Set a timer for 30 minutes and practice handstands and deadlifts. Kb swings at high reps can be rough for new kids
